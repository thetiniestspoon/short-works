---
title: Zoe
type: fantasy-fragment
---

Zoe looked back only once the day she left home forever. Having unceremoniously finished her house chores, she was happy to find the summer sun had stretched the afternoon long like dough in a baker's hands. She half-tied the knots in her dusty shoes and set off with a gallop across the open fields. Her course was set for the forest at the edge of the pastures. The wind raced past her and turned over the amber and emerald, making waves on the hillside and loosening her long hair from the tight bun behind her head. It was just as she was reaching the apex of a hill when she was suddenly struck by the feeling that something had been forgotten. Her jaunt from the house toward the distant tree line suddenly slowed to a halt. After a moment she dropped herself down on the soft earth, reclining in the long grass and letting her gaze roll upwards to the infinite blue. Her mind lolled through the long list of responsibilities she had left behind at the house, ensuring each was in fact finished. In truth, she found herself rather unmotivated to stay here much longer, drawn as she was by the lure of unimaginable adventures beyond the forest ahead. Yet Zoe felt for a brief second on the precipice of two wholly disparate lives, and was overwhelmed by an unmitigated longing to look homeward once more.

Pulling herself up to sit, she turned to find the house looking smaller than she had remembered. The squat grey building stood atop a far-off hill, resting languorously amidst the spirited dance of the surrounding countryside. Linens billowed on the clothesline like the sails of a ship anchored on a blue-green ocean. Zoe vowed that she would be home by dinner and rose to her feet, brushing the grass from her faded pink dress. She turned back to face the forest and jettisoned all thoughts of the life behind her.

With reckless abandon, she ran into the forest, trudging forth over the earth riddled with stones and weeds. The trees stood tall, their branches swaying and dancing tangos while the Sun peeked through and cast light and shadow across her path. She continued along, her route growing steeper and darker with each step, until the Sun, once able to find its way down to the forest floor, now seemed swallowed up in the thickening knots of branches overhead. Zoe steadied herself on the gnarled roots that flanked her path, half pulling herself up the rising slope. Her breathing grew more labored with each step, and she soon realized that her dress had become damp with sweat. She surveyed the dark, encroaching forest for a comfortable spot to rest and spied a glimpse of sunlight not much further up the hill. She stepped unhurriedly through the brambles and brush toward the glowing oasis. As she moved closer, Zoe realized that she was approaching an opening in the tree line. Her pace quickened until she was flying toward the warm sunlight, and soon she erupted from the forest into a small clearing, at the center of which was a tree larger than any she had ever seen. Zoe fought to catch her breath, standing alone with the mighty tree in the otherwise empty clearing. The tree was encircled only by its own mighty roots, which grappled and hugged the earth. As Zoe looked up, she was amazed to see that the lowest boughs of the tree were miles above the ground. Its underbelly glowed a deep emerald green and seemed to pulsate as if with its own heartbeat. Zoe walked around the perimeter of the tree, climbing over the twisted roots that weaved over and under the earth. The magnitude of the trunk astonished her, its curve fairly negligible, and she half wondered if this wasn't some sort of strange dream. After nearly an hour, Zoe realized that she had only then returned to her starting point and dropped wearily against a nearby root. The Sun had dropped low in the sky and huddled just above the surrounding tree line, throwing the world into a pool of gold and orange.

Zoe let her head fall back against the root's side, wondering whether she should continue circling the trunk. The tree had turned out to be larger than she had anticipated entirely. Her eyes rose again to the dark green underbelly of the tree, and even in the low light, the wall of green that covered the lowest boughs still seemed to move and beat with life. As her eyes adjusted in the twilight, Zoe noticed that amongst the dark green above there was a hint of light coming from somewhere amongst the branches. She strained to make out some sort of detail and could just see a faint line running from the source of light down the trunk of the tree to the ground nearby. She quickly rose to her feet and navigated through the roots until she had reached the base of the line. It was a ladder carved right into the tree trunk, climbing straight up the tree towards what she was now certain was a glowing light coming from within the boughs.

The sheer anticipation of discovering the source of this strange light took hold of her, and before she had thought much further she was taking her first steps up the ladder. A part of her knew that this was a foolish idea. The ladder climbed so far up the tree and with nothing else to hold her, the climb was absolutely perilous. Yet she found herself taking rung after rung in hand, her feet following carefully below. Before long, she was high above the surrounding tree line and could see the Sun setting in the distance, peeking curiously over the horizon. She climbed ever higher, until the evening light had been sucked away and she was left alone, high in the cold night. Zoe clenched the rungs of the ladder tightly, determined not to look down even once. The wind roared furiously around her, ripping and pulling at her dress.

As she drew closer to the pulsating underbelly of the tree, Zoe found herself able to make out why the tree had appeared to move. Though it had appeared to be a solid wall of green from the ground, she could now see vines of ivy overlapping one another, creating a blanket that undulated and flowed as if riding waves on an inverted ocean. The ladder continued directly up the trunk of the tree, plunging purposefully through the ivy. Above her, the green fronds glowed brightly, lit from somewhere within the tree. She continued her climb, letting go only to push aside the fronds of ivy flowing past her. The ivy leaned and pushed back, pressing her tight against the ladder. She used the weight to hoist herself up with some ease, and Zoe soon found herself leaning almost entirely on a wall of ivy. As she pushed into the broad leaves, they pushed back, balancing the force seamlessly. They glowed gold and green around her as she swam through a sea of ivy, pushing armfuls aside, until she finally came upon the source of the light. A gas lantern hung from a wooden platform just a few feet above her head. The platform appeared to support a tree house, stretching deep into the ivy in every direction. Directly overhead, there was a circular door with a cast-iron doorknocker. The vines emanated from the cracks between the floorboards and smothered the light coming through from above. Over the murmurs of the ivy swaying in the wind below, Zoe was certain she could hear someone humming, and realizing that she had reached the absolute end of her climb, Zoe did the only thing that seemed reasonable at the time: she knocked.
